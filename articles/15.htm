A SOCIAL CLUB OF THE FORTY-NINTH.

The members of Company L, 49th U. S. Vols., have organized a social club in their company, at Claveria, under the jurisdiction of Capt. Edward L Baker, 49th Vols. The club is known as the "Old Honesty, Social and Beneficial Club of Company L... 49th Vols.," and was organized Sept. 1, 1900, with the following officers: Pres. Thomas Miller, Vice Pres. William Smothers, 2nd Vice-Pres. G. R. Glenn, Sec'ty. J. A. Woods, Asst. Sec'ty. J. W. Snow, Cor Sec'ty. C. Admire, Treas. S. Murphy, Asst. Treas. J. L. Rice, Chief Com. A. Seals, Gen. Mgr. T. E. Philipps, Asst. Gen. Mgr. E. Callian, Mas.-at-Arms J., Gross, Asst. Mas.-at-Arms S. Marter, Lib. A. Gray, Asst. Lib. C. A. Russell, Finance Com. J. Maddox, T. Simmons and J. Hudson.

The club gave an entertainment New Year's night that was a very enjoyable affair, and lasted from 7:30 to 11:30. All the troops stationed at Claveria and a large number of the prominent natives in that vicinity attended. Among those present. from the 16th Infantry, were; Lieut. C. W. Weeks, Corp. R. Ferguson, Privates Feagans and Wilks.
